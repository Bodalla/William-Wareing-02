About Me

My name is William Wareing, and I am currently in a rigorous and thorough course to learn coding, hoping to start a carreer in the field. My experiences range drastically in terms of work, providing well enough proof to his adaptability and versatility. My first job was as a lot porter at a car dealership, in charge of retrieving cars, and washing vehicles, assisting mechanics, and adjusting tire pressure. Fulfilling said job description required a great deal of priority-management, attention to detail, and overall awareness. After that, I went on to work in sober livings and addiction centers as a house manager, shift lead, client care coordinator, and support staff. In order to perform these roles adequately, I had to develop unrivaled empathy, patience, foresight, assertiveness, deescalation, and stress-management. After two and a half years of working in recovery, I was laid off due to the pandemic, and I decided that I wanted to take on new challenges. The next path I took was in door-to-door sales, yet I decided that was not the route I wanted to take. Lasting longer than most rookies in sales, I developed an aptitude for social skills and rejection-management. My next job was of a personal nature. I worked as a medical assistant and medical transport for my parents. After both were injured in separate accidents and more or less immobilized, I decided to step up and assist them. It was a full-time job in terms of hours, so no other job could be tacked onto the position. Now that they are able to walk once more, I have started a bootcamp for coding, and I set my sights in spending the remainder of my life in the field.

I am, furthermore, an aspiring novelist and creative writer. I have two published works, one poem and one short story, at the moment, and I plans to do more. I have various passion projects, utilizing my skills in what way I can. Ultimately, William is well-rounded, skilled, and determined to forge my future.
